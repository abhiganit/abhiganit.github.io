  • They assumed that vaccine effectiveness to be 80% against hospitalization and death and 50% against upper respiratory infection (similar to influenza vaccine effectiveness).
  • They discounted nursing home admissions and deaths following RSV-related lower respiratory infection, and nursing home and future medical costs at 3% for base and best case and at 5% for the worst case scenario.
  • They considered two scenarios:
    1. Without a 10% vaccine effectiveness against COPD,
    2. With a 10% vaccine effectiveness against COPD

Mortality Associated With Influenza and Respiratory Syncytial Virus in United States (Thompson et al. 2003 - JAMA)

  • This paper has information for elderly people at finer scales, a lot of it is about influenza related illness (worth a look, if required).
  • 78% of RSV-associated underlying respiratory and circulatory deaths occurred among persons age 65 years or older.

Respiratory Syncytial Virus Infection in Elderly and High-Risk Adults (Falsey et al. 2005 - N.Engl.J.Med.)

  • Among healthy elderly patients, influenza A infection generated a greater percentage of office visits/outpatient visits than did RSV infection (42% vs 17%)
  • The total number of health care visits (office visits, emergency room visits and hospitalizations) was similar for the two viruses.
  • Overall, RSV infection accounted for 10.6% of hospitalization for pneumonia, 11.4% for COPD, 5.4% for congestive heart failure and 7.2% for ashthma.
  • Combining data for no of discharges from U.S. hospitals of adults 65+ for the diagnose of pneumonia, COPD, congestive heart failure and asthma from National Center for Health Statistics of the CDC, in 1999 with their rates, they found that RSV infection would account for approximately 177,525 admissions each year.
  • Also, based on the death rate for hospitalized patients with RSV infection (8%), 14,000 annual deaths would be predicted.
  • 18% of outpatient/office visits made by elderly adults for respiratory illness during winter were RSV-related.

RSV vaccine in development: Assessing the potential cost-effectiveness in the Dutch elderly population (Meijboom et al. 2013 - Vaccine)

  • They have life expectancies for High and low risk individuals in age-groups (60-64,65-74,75-84,85+). High risk population was categorized based on International Classification of Primary Care (ICPC).
  • They have utilities of 0.46 for GP visit and 0.35 for hospitalization. Moreover, they assume that patients experience the utility for 1 week in case of GP visit and 3 weeks when they are hospitalized. (Is this right ?).
