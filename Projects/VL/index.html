2 Fitting

I fitted the parameters transmission risk \(\beta\), complete recovery rate \(\nu\) and relapse rate \(\sigma\) as well as three initial conditions using 'fminsearchbnd' in Matlab. For rest of the parameters and initial conditions, I either use estimates from literature or use an assumed value See Here.

I ran the estimation routine starting from \(32\) random initial guesses for the parameters between their lower and upper bound. I finally save the parameter estimates that maximizes the likelihood most as best parameter estimates.

3 Future work

We decided to use both simple and co-infection models without delay to evaluate if the treatment failure and relapse or/and HIV-VL co-infection reservoir poses any problems in achieving the goal of reducing the annual incidence to less that 1 case per 10,000 individuals by 2017 in the Indian state of Bihar.

3.1 Approach

For both simple and co-infection model, we run the model to equilibrium with estimated set of parameters. Once the model has reached equilibrium, we vary the relevant parameters to measure its impact on the endemicity of VL. Particularly,

  • We will vary relapse term for the simple model.
  • and in the co-infection model where we assume that relapse happens only in the co-infected individuals, we will vary the relapse term as well as co-infection level (term).

NOTE: We will compare our results for the models with and without delay as well if possible.
